What if equality looked equal — but wasn't?

Switchshift is a narrative simulation that flips gender roles in the workplace, inviting players to experience what structural bias feels like from the inside.

A Game About Work, Power, and Perspective

In Switchshift, the modern office becomes a mirror. Every interaction — from daily tasks to salary discussions — unfolds within a reversed social order where women hold structural power and men navigate subtle inequities.

Rather than telling players what to think, Switchshift lets them live the imbalance, revealing how systems that appear fair can still produce unfair outcomes.

The project combines narrative design, interactive storytelling, and real gender pay data to expose what bias looks like when the rules stay the same — but the roles change.

Inside a Familiar Yet Inverted World

Players move through a sequence of realistic workplace environments — offices, meetings, evaluations, negotiations — each reflecting a different layer of inequality.

Decisions are small, consequences subtle, but together they reveal how everyday systems quietly decide who gets heard, promoted, and paid.

Each moment is part of a larger structure: an evolving simulation about ambition, recognition, and the illusion of meritocracy.

Flat. Minimal. Designed for Discomfort.

The world of Switchshift is drawn in flat illustration style.

Every visual element reflects the system it critiques: clean, ordered, and quietly hierarchical.

• Warm gold represents privilege and authority.
• Cool blue represents restraint and invisibility.
• Together, they form a balanced palette that never truly balances.

Experience the World of Switchshift

Switchshift is not about winning — it's about seeing. It turns everyday career moments into a reflective experience of power, perception, and bias.

The prototype presents the core gameplay and tone of a larger world currently in development. Players are invited to question not only the game's system, but the one we all share.
